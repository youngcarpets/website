# Sheet Vinyl

## What it is

Sheet vinyl is the right answer when joints are the enemy. Operating theatres, ICUs, isolation rooms, dental surgeries, commercial kitchens, dialysis bays, lab spaces, pharmacy compounding suites — anywhere a continuous, heat-welded, coved-to-the-wall surface is what the spec calls for.

The defining detail is the **integral flash cove base**: the sheet rolls up the wall over a cove former, the top edge is capped, and the floor/wall corner — the hardest spot in any room to keep clean — disappears. No horizontal seam, no harbourage for bacteria, no mop-line failure, no grout to fail.

When The Ottawa Hospital, CHEO, the Royal, Montfort, Queensway Carleton or Bruyère issues a flooring spec for an OR, an isolation suite, a central sterile processing department or a cytotoxic compounding room, it is almost always a heat-welded, flash-coved homogeneous sheet vinyl or linoleum assembly. This is the Canadian healthcare default, and YCI has been installing it since 1991.

## The cove-build sequence — defining detail

The marketing site renders this as a five-step animated sequence. Each step is a real, distinct phase of the install:

1. **Bare wall + slab.** Subfloor is prepped, moisture-tested (ASTM F1869 / F2170), flat to ≤3/16" in 10', and primed. Cove formers (wood, aluminum or purpose-made PVC) are mechanically fastened at the floor/wall junction in a continuous radius — typically 1" to 1-1/4".
2. **Sheet rolls down.** Full-spread adhesive is trowelled onto the slab per manufacturer notched-trowel spec, the sheet is unrolled, relaxed, and rolled with a 100 lb three-section roller to bed the sheet in the adhesive and expel air.
3. **Sheet curves up the wall.** At the perimeter, the material is scribed, relief-cut at outside corners, heat-softened if required, and pressed up and over the cove former and flat onto the wall — typically 4" to 6" above the finished floor. Inside corners are patched with a cut-in gusset and welded in.
4. **Cap snaps in.** The top edge of the coved sheet is terminated. Depending on spec, this is either a vinyl cap strip (aluminum or PVC receiver) mechanically fastened and snapped over the cut edge, or the sheet is scribed tight to a hardwall base above and sealed.
5. **Weld line traces.** Field and cove seams are allowed to cure (typically 24 hours), then routed with a groover to a specific depth (roughly two-thirds the sheet thickness), cleaned, and heat-welded with a colour-matched PVC welding rod using a hot-air welding gun at ~400–450 °C. Once cooled, the bead is skive-trimmed flush with a mooncutter, then finish-trimmed with a spatula knife. The result is a monolithic, chemically-fused membrane — floor, cove, seam and all.

This is what distinguishes a healthcare-grade sheet install from a commercial LVT install, and it is the single most skilled phase of resilient flooring work YCI does.

## Types / sub-products

### Heterogeneous sheet
Printed or decorative pattern layer laminated under a clear PVC wear layer, over a backing (foam, felt, glass-fibre or compact). Allows for wood-look, stone-look, patterned and branded designs. Wear layer typically 0.55 mm to 1.0 mm. Governed by **ASTM F1303**. Common in schools, long-term care, lobbies, back-of-house hospital corridors, staff areas.

### Homogeneous sheet
Colour and structure run through the entire thickness of the sheet — no separate wear layer, no delamination risk. The healthcare default. Typically 2.0 mm total thickness. Same material family as SCT (Solid Colour Tile), but in continuous roll form. Governed by **ASTM F1700** (solid vinyl) and EN ISO 10581. Tarkett iQ Granit and Forbo Sphera are the category references.

### Linoleum (Forbo Marmoleum)
Natural linoleum — linseed oil, pine rosin, wood flour, limestone, jute backing. Not vinyl at all, but installed with identical technique (heat-welded seams, flash cove). Naturally bacteriostatic, BREEAM A+ rated, 25+ year lifecycle. Preferred for LEED/WELL projects and pediatric facilities (CHEO spec favourite).

### Safety (slip-resistant) sheet
PVC wear layer with aluminium oxide, silicon carbide, and/or quartz particles embedded throughout the thickness. R-rated per DIN 51130 (R10 / R11 / R12) and PTV-tested per EN 13893. Altro Safety Flooring is the Canadian healthcare and commercial-kitchen default. Used anywhere the floor is expected to be wet during normal operation: dish pits, shower rooms, decant areas, labs, kennels, fish prep. Thicker (2.0–3.0 mm+) than standard homogeneous sheet.

### Static-dissipative / conductive (ESD)
Specialty homogeneous sheet with embedded carbon fibres and installed over a copper grounding grid, terminated at an earth point. For clean rooms, electronics assembly, imaging suites, OR where electrosurgical equipment or flammable anaesthetics are in use. Tarkett **iQ Granit SD**, Forbo **Sphera SD / Sphera EC**, Gerflor **Mipolam Biocontrol ESD**. *(Verify current Ontario availability per project.)*

### Acoustic-backed sheet
Homogeneous or heterogeneous sheet laminated to a closed-cell foam backing (typically 19 dB impact noise reduction). Used in multi-storey healthcare and education where footfall transmission is a concern. Not appropriate for wet zones or heavy rolling loads. Tarkett **iQ Granit Acoustic** is the reference product.

## Install techniques

| Technique | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Heat-welded seams** | Seams routed with a manual or electric groover to ~2/3 sheet thickness. Colour-matched PVC welding rod melted into the groove with a hot-air welder at 400–450 °C. Bead skived flush in two passes (hot skive with mooncutter + cooled finish skive). Forms a chemically-fused, monolithic, waterproof membrane. Non-negotiable in healthcare. |
| **Flash coving (integral cove base)** | Sheet rolled up the wall 4"–6" over a 1"–1-1/4" radius cove former. Relief cuts at outside corners, welded gussets at inside corners. Terminated with a PVC/aluminum cap strip or scribed to hardwall base. Eliminates the floor/wall horizontal seam entirely. **The single defining feature of healthcare flooring.** |
| **Full-spread adhesive** | Wet-set or pressure-sensitive per manufacturer. Troweled with the manufacturer's specified notch (commonly 1/16" U-notch for sheet). Rolled with a 100 lb three-section roller in both directions within adhesive open time. No loose-lay, no click, no perimeter-only. |
| **Double-cut seams** | Adjacent sheets overlapped ~1", both layers cut through in a single pass with a long straightedge, scrap pulled, seam dressed into adhesive. Produces a gap-free fit for welding. |
| **Substrate prep** | Mechanical prep (shot-blast, grind, or diamond-cup), patching with Portland-based leveler, embossing leveler over existing VCT if asbestos-suspect and encapsulation is approved. Moisture testing and pH testing before adhesive hits the floor. |
| **Cove former installation** | Wood, aluminum or extruded-PVC cove formers mechanically fastened to base of wall before sheet goes down. Must be continuous, tight to slab, and radiused — a faceted or gapped former telegraphs through the finished cove. |

## Why sheet over tile in these environments

- **No tile joints, no grout, no microbial harbourage.** A 10,000 sqft OR corridor has zero linear feet of tile joint — only a handful of heat-welded seams, each a continuous plasticised weld bead.
- **No water ingress.** The assembly is effectively a welded membrane continuous up the wall.
- **Single continuous cleaning surface.** Critical for infection prevention and control (IPC) programs, and for spray-and-wipe disinfection protocols.
- **Coved walls eliminate the floor/wall corner.** This corner is the hardest spot to keep clean in any room, and the first place mop water fails.
- **Heat-welded seams meet infection-control standards** per CSA Z8000 (Canadian Health Care Facilities — Planning, Design and Construction) and CSA Z317.13 (Infection Control During Construction, Renovation and Maintenance of Health Care Facilities).
- **Rolling load tolerance.** Gurneys, heavy carts, imaging equipment — homogeneous sheet is rated for sustained rolling loads that will shred LVT seams.
- **Decontamination rating.** Tarkett iQ Granit is rated "Excellent" to ISO 8690 / DIN 25415 (ease of decontamination of radioactive surface contamination).

## Best NOT used for

- Design-led showroom and boutique retail spaces (use LVT, wood or polished concrete)
- Residential interiors where seam weld lines would read as institutional
- Long unbroken corridors wider than ~40' without relief seams (substrate flatness and roll-width become prohibitive)
- Anywhere the maintenance crew won't follow an OR/ICU sanitization protocol (the material will outlast the abuse, but it won't look the part)
- Over uncured or high-moisture concrete without a moisture-mitigation system
- Outdoor, unconditioned, or freeze-thaw environments

## Substrate prep — critical specs

Sheet vinyl is less forgiving than LVT. Every substrate defect telegraphs through the finished membrane, and every heat-welded seam is a microscope trained on the subfloor.

- **Moisture test required** on all concrete: ASTM F1869 (CaCl, max 3 lb / 1000 sqft / 24 hr typical) or ASTM F2170 (in-situ RH probe, max 75–85% RH depending on manufacturer). No exceptions.
- **pH test** of the slab surface, typically acceptable range 7–9.
- **Flatness ≤ 3/16" in 10'** and ≤ 1/16" in 1' — *tighter than LVT*. Any ridge telegraphs through a 2 mm homogeneous sheet under side-lighting.
- **Cleanliness** — no curing compounds, sealers, paint, old adhesive residue or drywall dust. Mechanical prep (shot blast, grind) is the norm.
- **Patching** with a Portland-based cementitious patch compatible with the finish adhesive. Do not use gypsum-based patches.
- **Embossing leveler over old VCT** where the existing VCT is sound, adhered and asbestos-suspect — do NOT remove old VCT in occupied healthcare buildings without a hazardous-materials assessment, an abatement plan and containment per O. Reg. 278/05 (Ontario Designated Substance — Asbestos).
- **Cove formers** installed *before* the sheet goes down. Continuous, tight, radiused.
- **Transitions** — pre-planned reducer strips or heat-welded flush transitions to adjacent finishes.

## Maintenance

- **Daily:** dust mop, then damp mop with a neutral-pH cleaner. No high-alkaline strippers on homogeneous sheet — they attack the PVC surface treatment.
- **Periodic (2–12 weeks):** spray-buff or low-speed burnish with a red or white pad. Tarkett iQ, Forbo Sphera and Gerflor Mipolam with factory PUR surface treatment do **not** require waxing or polish application — a buff restores the factory finish.
- **Healthcare disinfection:** follow the facility's OR/ICU protocol. Quat-ammonium and accelerated hydrogen peroxide (AHP) cleaners are compatible with all major sheet products. Phenolics and bleach at in-use dilution are generally acceptable — confirm with the manufacturer IPC datasheet for the specific product.
- **Safety flooring (Altro, Tarasafe, Polysafe):** scrub with a deck brush or rotary machine and a blue pad — the slip-resistant particles require mechanical agitation, not just mop pressure. Do NOT burnish safety flooring — it destroys the slip rating.
- **Linoleum (Marmoleum):** use only Marmoleum-approved alkaline-neutral cleaners; linoleum is sensitive to high-alkaline products. Factory Topshield pro finish is maintenance-free for the first years.
- **Repair:** heat-weld patch with retained scrap. The seam is routed out, a new patch is cut to the scribed profile, adhered, and rewelded. Invisible if done properly — the single biggest advantage of heat-welded sheet over LVT.
- **Lifecycle:** 15–25 years typical in commercial healthcare; Marmoleum and top-tier homogeneous lines routinely deliver 25–30 years with proper maintenance.
