# Rubber

> Three sub-segments under one product card: **fitness**, **healthcare**, **industrial**. The marketing site uses a 3-segment toggle modal — each segment gets equal depth because the buyer personas, specs, and brands do not overlap.

## What it is

High-density vulcanized rubber flooring for environments where impact absorption, slip resistance, sound dampening, ergonomic comfort, or chemical resistance matter more than pure aesthetics. Unlike vinyl, rubber is a true elastomer — it compresses under load and rebounds, which is why it owns the weight room and the nurses' station. Modern commercial rubber is PVC-free, phthalate-free, halogen-free, and most premium lines carry recycled content (post-industrial EPDM or post-consumer tire crumb).

Rubber comes in three basic forms that span all three segments:
- **Sheet** — rolls, typically 48–74" wide, glue-down, butted or heat/cold-welded seams
- **Tile** — 19.69" × 19.69" or 24" × 24", dry-lay interlock or full glue-down
- **Poured-in-place** — granulated rubber + polyurethane binder, troweled seamless on site

## FITNESS

### Sub-products

| Sub-product | Typical thickness | Install | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Interlocking tile | 8–12mm (high-density vulcanized) | Dry-lay, perimeter glue at walls | Free-weight floors, functional training zones |
| Rolled rubber sheet | 6–10mm | Full glue-down, butted seams | Cardio decks, group fitness studios, selectorized machine areas |
| Poured-in-place | 1/4"–3/8" (6–10mm), custom up to 1/2" | Granulated EPDM + PU binder, troweled on site | Seamless deck under platforms, logo inlays, irregular footprints |
| Drop-zone pads | 2"–4" thick (50–100mm) | Drop-in or set into recessed slab | Under Olympic lifting platforms, dumbbell drop zones |

### Why rubber wins in fitness
- **Shock absorption** — protects both the slab and the athlete; ASTM F2772 force-reduction values of 13–17% for premium sport rubber
- **Dumbbell + barbell tolerance** — the only resilient surface that survives a 100 lb dumbbell drop at >8mm thickness
- **Sound attenuation** — critical in multi-tenant buildings and stacked fitness levels; dense rubber + acoustic underlayment can hit IIC >55
- **Slip safety when wet** — R9/R10 ratings standard, higher than most LVT in the same space
- **Aesthetic flexibility** — pigmented EPDM fleck, custom logo inlays, colour-matched branding for corporate and university clients

### Key install details
- **Concrete moisture** — CCI moisture test required on slab-on-grade; premium glue-downs spec ≤5 lbs/1000 sf/24 hr MVER or install with a moisture-mitigation primer
- **Flatness** — ≤3/16" in 10' for sheet and tile; tighter (1/8" in 10') for poured-in-place under competitive lifting platforms
- **Substrate prep** — shot-blast or diamond-grind for full glue-down; prime with manufacturer-approved primer
- **Seam method** — butted for tile and most rolled sheet; heat-weld rod available on premium Mondo and Regupol lines for cardio wet zones
- **Drop-zone recess** — coordinate with GC early; 2" recessed slab preferred so platform sits flush with surrounding deck

## HEALTHCARE

### Sub-products

| Sub-product | Typical thickness | Install | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sheet rubber, heat-welded | 2mm or 3mm | Full glue-down, heat-weld rod at seams | Patient rooms, corridors, nurses' stations |
| Integral coved base | Same sheet rolled up wall 4–6" | Cove former, top cap strip | Wet zones, pharmacy, clean rooms, infection-control corridors |
| Static-dissipative (ED) | 2mm or 3mm | Glue-down with conductive adhesive + copper grounding strip | MRI suites, imaging, electronics labs, server rooms, ORs |
| Acoustic-backed | 4mm total (2mm wear + 2mm acoustic backing) | Glue-down | Long-term care, inpatient rooms where ΔIIC matters |

### Why rubber wins in healthcare (the "why" copy)

**1. Underfoot comfort over 12-hour shifts.** Nurses and ICU staff walk 4–5 miles a shift on hard slabs. Rubber's natural elasticity reduces lower-back and knee fatigue in a way vinyl simply cannot — it's the single biggest reason Canadian health authorities keep specifying premium rubber despite the cost premium.

**2. Acoustic performance for patient rest.** Sheet rubber delivers dramatically better footfall-sound absorption than sheet vinyl or LVT. Nora publishes footfall sound absorption values up to 20 dB — the difference between "I can hear the overnight nurse's shoes" and "I can't." Sleep is a measured patient outcome.

**3. Continuous waterproof membrane.** Heat-welded seams + integral coved base create a single impermeable surface from wall to wall with no horizontal floor-to-wall seam where pathogens can harbour — this is the configuration infection-control consultants specify under **CAN/CSA Z317.13** (Infection control during construction, renovation, and maintenance of health care facilities, 2022 5th edition) and **CSA Z317.12** (Cleaning and disinfection of health care facilities).

**4. Chemical resistance to hospital cleaners.** Premium rubber resists the quaternary ammonium compounds, accelerated hydrogen peroxide (AHP), and bleach dilutions used in Canadian hospital cleaning protocols. It does not scuff-mark from wheelchair tires or bed-transport the way vinyl composition tile does.

**5. Dimensional stability over heated slabs.** Healthcare facilities increasingly use radiant-slab heating. Rubber does not telegraph seams, cup, or gap the way some sheet vinyls do over thermal cycling.

**6. PVC-free, phthalate-free, halogen-free.** Premium lines (Nora, Artigo, Mondo Harmoni) are fully free of PVC and chlorine — no dioxin release if the building burns, and no plasticizer off-gassing. Aligns with LEED, WELL v2, and the increasingly common "Red List Free" specs on Canadian healthcare RFPs.

**7. Static-dissipative option in one product family.** The same aesthetic and install logic carries into ESD zones (imaging, OR, electronics) — you can spec one brand across the whole hospital.

### Key install details
- **Concrete prep** — shot-blast or diamond-grind; moisture test mandatory; 28-day minimum cure on new slabs
- **Flatness** — ≤3/16" in 10' minimum; sheet rubber is unforgiving of slab humps and will telegraph
- **Adhesive** — manufacturer-specific two-part PU or specialized acrylic; conductive adhesive for ED lines
- **Seams** — heat-weld rod in matching or contrasting colour; some lines (Nora noraplan sentica) can also cold-weld for smaller projects
- **Coved base** — cove former (typically 4" radius) screwed to wall; sheet rolls up and over; top capped with colour-matched aluminum or vinyl cap strip
- **ESD** — copper grounding strip laid under sheet at specified spacing, terminated at building electrical ground per **ANSI/ESD S20.20**; field-test resistance (1×10⁴ to 1×10⁹ ohms typical dissipative range)

## INDUSTRIAL

### Sub-products

| Sub-product | Typical thickness | Install | Best use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anti-fatigue matting (drop-in) | 3/8"–3/4" | Loose-lay, modular interlock | Workstations, pharmacy compounding, assembly benches |
| Tile or sheet rubber (slip-rated, oil-resistant) | 3–6mm | Full glue-down | Mech rooms, light manufacturing, transit mezzanines |
| Stair treads + risers | 1/8"–1/4" wear layer, with cast-in nosing | Glue-down with contact cement or epoxy | School, transit, parking garage, office tower stairwells |
| Wall base (matching) | 2.5"–6" heights | Adhered | Paired with sheet or tile installs |

### Why rubber wins in industrial
- **Slip resistance under oil and water** — purpose-compounded industrial rubber holds R10/R11 ratings even in light oil contamination
- **Anti-fatigue ergonomics** — measurable reduction in worker lower-back and leg fatigue at workstations (WSIB-relevant in Ontario manufacturing)
- **Impact + abrasion resistance** — survives dropped tools, dollies, forklifts where LVT would dent and VCT would shatter
- **Sound dampening** — drops mech-room reverb that drives adjacent occupants crazy
- **Stair-safety code compliance** — cast-in abrasive nosings in contrasting colour hit the visual-contrast requirements of OBC and CSA B651 barrier-free standards
- **Chemical resistance** — handles mild oils, solvents, coolants, cleaning chemistries common in industrial environments

### Key install details
- **Stair treads** — measure each tread individually (stairs are never square); pre-cut and dry-fit entire run before gluing; contact cement or 2-part epoxy; roll firmly; cast-in aluminum-oxide or colour-contrast nosing strip is part of the moulded tread, not a separate piece
- **Risers** — cut from coordinating rubber sheet, glued to riser face, butted tight to tread back edge
- **Substrate (stairs)** — concrete pans must be dry, dust-free, and flat; grind high spots, fill low spots with cementitious patch
- **Anti-fatigue mats** — these are typically supply-only, not installed; measure workstation footprint, confirm beveled-edge safety requirement
- **Tile/sheet in mech rooms** — coordinate with mechanical trades; do not install until all overhead work is complete and slab is dry

## Substrate prep

- **Moisture test required** on all slab-on-grade: ASTM F2170 RH probe preferred (≤75% RH for most rubber), F1869 CaCl MVER as alternate (≤5 lbs/1000 sf/24 hr typical)
- **pH test** — surface pH ≤9 for most adhesives
- **Flatness** — ≤3/16" in 10' for sheet, tile; tighter for poured-in-place
- **Concrete cure** — 28 days minimum for new pours before any moisture test is valid
- **Prep method** — shot-blast or diamond-grind for glue-downs; vacuum before priming
- **Prime** — manufacturer-approved primer; never skip on polished or sealed slabs
- **Acclimation** — roll out sheet material in the space 24–48 hr before install at target temperature (15–27 °C) and RH

## Maintenance

- **Daily:** dust mop, neutral-pH cleaner damp mop
- **Weekly:** auto-scrub with soft white pad, neutral cleaner
- **Periodic:** low-speed buff; deeper scrub with manufacturer-approved cleaner as traffic demands
- **AVOID:** solvent-based cleaners, oil soaps, harsh acids/alkalis, abrasive pads on smooth finishes, steam cleaning on heat-welded seams
- **Stair treads:** spot-clean nosing inserts; inspect for lifted edges quarterly; replace damaged treads individually (no need to re-do full run)
- **Healthcare:** confirm all cleaners against the manufacturer chemical-compatibility chart before facility adopts a new cleaning chemistry
- **Lifecycle:** 15–30 years commercial typical; premium lines (Nora, Mondo) routinely hit 30+ in Canadian hospitals
