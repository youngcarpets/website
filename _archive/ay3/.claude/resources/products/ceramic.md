# Ceramic / Porcelain Tile

## What it is
Ceramic and porcelain tile for commercial entrances, lobbies, washrooms, kitchens, healthcare wet zones, pool decks, and heritage restoration. YCI installs to TTMAC standards over cement board, uncoupling membranes (Schluter Ditra), and waterproofing systems (Schluter Kerdi), with Ditra-Heat electric underlayment where specified. Large-format porcelain (≥15") is installed per TTMAC large-format protocol: back-buttered, ≥95% mortar coverage, lippage-controlled, with movement joints at the perimeter and every 20-25 ft interior grid.

Ceramic is the right answer when the floor needs to be hard, waterproof, chemically inert, and last 30-50+ years. It is the wrong answer when the room needs warmth underfoot or acoustic dampening.

## Types / sub-products

| Sub-type | Water absorption | Typical use |
|---|---|---|
| **Porcelain** | ≤ 0.5% (vitreous, per ANSI A137.1) | Commercial traffic, wet zones, exterior (freeze-thaw) |
| **Ceramic (glazed)** | 0.5% – 10% (semi-vitreous to non-vitreous) | Interior walls, light residential floors |
| **Large-format porcelain** | ≤ 0.5% | Lobbies, corridors, minimalist grid — tile ≥15"×15" up to 48"×48"+ and gauged porcelain panels to 60"×120" |
| **Mosaic (sheet-mounted)** | Varies | Shower floors, pool curves, drain slopes, decorative banding — pieces ≤2" |
| **Slip-rated wet-zone / outdoor** | ≤ 0.5% porcelain | Pool decks, change rooms, commercial kitchens, exterior stairs — R11 minimum |
| **Through-body porcelain** | ≤ 0.5% | Heavy commercial; colour runs full thickness so chips don't show |
| **Gauged porcelain panels (GPP/GPT)** | ≤ 0.5% | Feature walls, elevator lobbies, reception faces — 3mm-6mm thin panels up to 5'×10' |

## Install techniques

| Technique | Substrate | Best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Thinset bond coat | Concrete, cement board, Ditra | Most commercial work | ANSI A118.4 / A118.15 modified dryset; trowel sized to tile |
| Mortar bed (mud-set) | Concrete | Heritage restoration, slope-to-drain, shower pans | TTMAC Detail 301F/311F; thick-bed floated slope |
| Large-format protocol | Concrete, Ditra, cement board | Any tile ≥15" on any side | Back-butter mandatory; ≥95% mortar coverage wet areas, ≥80% dry areas; TTMAC large-format detail |
| Schluter Ditra | Concrete, plywood | Crack isolation + vapour management | ANSI A118.12 uncoupling; replaces cement board on most commercial floors |
| Schluter Kerdi | Cement board, drywall, framing | Showers, wet rooms, decontamination | ANSI A118.10 waterproofing sheet; bonded-flange drains |
| Schluter Ditra-Heat | Concrete, plywood | Electric heated floors | Uncoupling + heating cable in one mat; WiFi thermostat |
| Dilex movement joints | All | Required transitions | TTMAC 301MJ — perimeter, every 20-25 ft interior, over structural joints, at dissimilar substrates |
| Cove base tile | Concrete, Kerdi-Board | Commercial kitchens, healthcare | Sanitary cove with bullnose top; replaces vinyl cove in wet zones |

## Install patterns

| Pattern | Use case | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Stack (grid) | Modern minimal lobbies, large-format | Requires dead-flat substrate — any lippage reads immediately |
| Offset 1/3 brick | Large-format (≥18"), corridors | **Required** by most large-format manufacturers — 1/2 offset causes centre lippage on long tiles due to crown |
| Offset 1/2 brick | Smaller tiles (≤12"), subway walls | Classic pattern; avoid on long planks |
| Herringbone | Entry features, accent bands | Labour add — plan ~20% cut waste |
| Large-format single (butt joint) | Gauged panels, feature walls | Minimal grout, maximum visual slab |
| Mosaic sheet | Shower floors, curves, drains | Only format that follows a slope without lippage |

## Slip ratings — DIN 51130 (oil-wetted ramp test, shod foot)

**IMPORTANT:** R-ratings are ranges, not single points. Specs that cite a single angle are incomplete.

| Rating | Tilt angle | Commercial use |
|---|---|---|
| **R9** | 6° – 10° | Interior dry areas — office lobbies above grade, retail sales floors, corridors where no water, oil, or foot traffic from outside is expected |
| **R10** | > 10° – 19° | Washrooms (public and staff), small kitchens, staff cafeterias, entry areas **past** the mat-off zone, change rooms (dry side) |
| **R11** | > 19° – 27° | Commercial kitchens, laundries, food processing, entry mat-off zones in snowbelt climates, covered exterior entries, pool change rooms (wet side), loading docks under cover |
| **R12** | > 27° – 35° | Wet food processing, meat and fish prep, dairy, healthcare wet zones (decontamination, hydrotherapy), pool surrounds |
| **R13** | > 35° | Abattoirs, grease-heavy industrial kitchens, chemical processing with standing slurry, rendering |

**Ottawa-specific note:** Building entrances in the National Capital Region see sand, salt slurry, and ice for ~5 months a year. YCI specs **R11 minimum** for entry tile inside the mat-off zone, with proper walk-off matting (see `specialty.md`) in front of it. R10 is only acceptable well inside the building.

Complementary tests often cited in specs:
- **DIN 51097** — barefoot wet ramp test, classes A / B / C (pool decks, change rooms)
- **ASTM C1028** — static coefficient of friction *(withdrawn 2014; still referenced in legacy specs)*
- **ANSI A326.3** — DCOF AcuTest, ≥ 0.42 wet for level interior (current North American reference)

## Best used for
- Building entrances and lobbies (paired with R11 tile inside mat-off + proper walk-off matting)
- Washrooms — institutional, commercial, public, schools
- Hospital wet zones: decontamination, hydrotherapy, dialysis bays, morgues
- Commercial kitchens and food prep — R11 or higher, with cove base and floor drains
- Pool decks, change rooms, shower rooms — R11+ minimum, Kerdi waterproofing mandatory
- Heritage restoration where original flooring is tile or terrazzo-adjacent
- Elevator cabs and lobbies (porcelain only — ceramic chips)
- Exterior covered entries and balconies (porcelain only — freeze-thaw resistance)

## Best NOT used for
- Long office corridors — cold underfoot, no acoustic benefit, hard on staff on their feet all day
- Open-plan office workstation zones — noise and fatigue
- Classrooms, daycares — see sheet vinyl or rubber
- Anywhere acoustic dampening is a priority
- Raised access floors (tile weight + deflection issues)
- Gymnasium floors (wrong impact profile; see sports flooring)

## Substrate prep (TTMAC-aligned)
- **Concrete:** minimum 28 days cured, sound, dry, structurally stable. Moisture test per ASTM F1869 (CaCl) or F2170 (RH) if applying moisture-sensitive membranes.
- **Flatness:** TTMAC requires ≤ 1/4" in 10 ft for tile < 15"; **≤ 1/8" in 10 ft for tile ≥ 15"**. Large-format on an out-of-tolerance slab = lippage and hollow spots. Self-leveller first if needed.
- **Crack isolation:** Schluter Ditra or equivalent ANSI A118.12 membrane over any slab with existing cracks or crack-prone conditions. Mandatory over slab-on-grade with control joints crossing the tile field.
- **Movement joints:** per TTMAC detail 301MJ — continuous around perimeter, over every structural/control joint, at changes of plane, at dissimilar substrates, and at maximum 20-25 ft interior grid (exterior and direct-sun interior: 8-12 ft).
- **Large-format (≥15"):** back-butter mandatory. Minimum **95% mortar coverage in wet/exterior areas, 80% dry interior** — verified by periodic tile lift during install. Large-notch trowel (1/2" × 1/2" square or 3/4" U-notch).
- **Waterproofing:** Kerdi or Kerdi-Board over all wet-area framing before tile. Drain integration via Kerdi-Drain bonded flange. No exceptions in showers, decontamination, or hydrotherapy.
- **Heated floors:** Ditra-Heat cables in uncoupling mat, embedded in unmodified mortar, covered by bond coat. Thermostat with floor + ambient sensor.

## Maintenance
- **Daily:** dust mop or micro-fibre; neutral pH cleaner damp mop. Never acid cleaners on cement grout.
- **Weekly:** auto-scrubber with neutral detergent in commercial kitchens; grout brush attachment as needed.
- **Periodic (quarterly–annual):** alkaline deep clean on grout lines; steam clean in washrooms and kitchens.
- **Sealing:**
  - **Epoxy grout** (Mapei Kerapoxy, Laticrete SpectraLOCK) — no resealing ever. Specified in wet zones, kitchens, hospitals.
  - **Cement grout** — reseal every 1-3 years with penetrating silane/siloxane sealer. Never film-forming.
- **Repair:** pop damaged tile with oscillating tool, grind out grout, re-set with matching mortar. **Always retain field tile from original install** — dye lots shift and matches become impossible after 2-3 years.
- **Lifecycle:** 30-50+ years commercial. Grout typically needs regrouting at 15-25 years; tile itself outlasts the building envelope.
