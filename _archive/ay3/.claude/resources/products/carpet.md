# Carpet (broadloom)

## What it is
Rolled commercial carpet — wall-to-wall textile flooring delivered on 6 ft or 12 ft wide rolls, seamed in place on site. The right answer for institutional, corporate, hospitality and assembly environments where uninterrupted visual flow, acoustic dampening, warmth-underfoot, and long continuous sightlines matter more than the replaceability of a modular tile.

Broadloom is specified when the design intent is "one continuous surface" — ballrooms, auditoriums, lecture halls, long government corridors, ecclesiastical spaces. When the client needs patchable wear zones (open-plan offices with hot desks, school classrooms on tight replacement budgets), carpet tile is usually the better call — see `carpet-tile.md`.

## Types / sub-products
- **Direct glue-down broadloom** — unitary or ActionBac-style back, adhesive trowelled to substrate, carpet rolled and seam-sealed. Lowest profile, highest dimensional stability, lowest cost per sq ft installed. The Ontario commercial default.
- **Cushion-backed (attached-cushion) broadloom** — factory-laminated cushion layer on the back of the carpet; single-step glue-down. Adds acoustic value and underfoot comfort without the labour of a true double-stick.
- **Double-stick system (separate cushion + broadloom)** — commercial-grade bonded urethane cushion glued to the slab, broadloom glued to the cushion. Premium acoustic and ergonomic performance; used in ballrooms, boardrooms, executive floors.
- **Woven broadloom** (Axminster, Wilton) — high-end patterned goods, typical in hospitality ballrooms, theatres, and heritage government spaces. Long lead times, custom colour runs.
- **Tufted broadloom** — the workhorse of commercial: faster to mill, lower cost, broad pattern and construction range.
- **Custom-coloured / custom-patterned** — short-run mill orders for design teams; typical minimums 500–1,000 sq yd depending on mill.
- **Integrated walk-off broadloom** — entrance-rated broadloom with grit-trapping construction for vestibules and transition zones.

## Fibre systems
Almost all commercial broadloom in the Canadian market is nylon. The meaningful distinctions:

| Fibre | Typical use | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Nylon 6,6 (Antron by INVISTA, successor programs) | Class A offices, government, long-life spec work | Denser molecular structure, best wear recovery, best colour retention; typically solution-dyed (Antron Lumena) for commercial |
| Nylon 6 (Universal, Aquafil ECONYL, Shaw EcoSolution Q) | Corporate, education, hospitality | Easier to dye, vivid colourways; ECONYL and EcoSolution Q are regenerated / closed-loop nylon programs |
| Solution-dyed PET / polyester | Budget corporate, interim spaces | Excellent stain resistance, lower wear recovery than nylon; not a Class A spec |
| Wool / wool-nylon blends | Heritage, ecclesiastical, executive | Premium Axminster / Wilton woven; natural crush recovery, cost and lead time scale accordingly |

**Solution-dyed vs. piece-dyed:** solution-dyed fibre has pigment extruded into the polymer — colour is locked in and effectively bleach-cleanable. For any healthcare-adjacent or heavy-traffic institutional spec, solution-dyed is the default recommendation.

## Install techniques
| Technique | Substrate | Best for | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct glue-down | Concrete or plywood | Offices, corridors, classrooms, retail | Most stable, lowest profile, CRI 104-compliant. The Ontario commercial standard. |
| Double-stick (separate cushion + carpet) | Concrete | Ballrooms, boardrooms, executive floors | Acoustic + comfort, two adhesive lines, two cure cycles; premium labour |
| Cushion-back single-step glue-down | Concrete or plywood | Mid-tier offices, education | Factory cushion eliminates second glue line; faster install than true double-stick |
| Stretch-in over pad (tackstrip perimeter) | Plywood or concrete with tackstrip anchors | Executive suites, residential-feel offices | Uncommon in modern commercial spec; used where underfoot comfort is non-negotiable and re-stretch access is acceptable |
| Heat-welded / sewn seams | Any | Hospitality, woven goods, >100 ft² runs | Invisible joins between drops; sewn is standard for high-end Axminster |
| Seam sealing | All glue-down | Required by CRI 104 on every cut edge | Latex or thermoplastic seam sealer on trimmed edges to prevent fraying and edge ravel |

All commercial broadloom installation in Canada should reference **CRI 104 — Standard for Installation of Commercial Carpet**. CRI 104 defines substrate prep, trowel notch, adhesive open time, seam layout, roll direction, acclimation, and moisture requirements. Any spec that doesn't name CRI 104 is incomplete.

**Acclimation:** broadloom should be delivered and rolled out (or stood on end) in the conditioned space for a minimum of 24 hours at 18–35 °C / 10–65% RH before cutting. Adhesive cure requires substrate temperature ≥ 18 °C for 72 hours before, during, and after install.

**Roll direction:** all drops must run the same direction (arrows on back of carpet). Reversing a drop to save waste will produce a visible shade variation under raking light — one of the most common site callbacks.

## Install patterns
Broadloom is roll goods — you don't "pattern" the install the way you do with carpet tile (ashlar, brick, quarter-turn). The visual pattern comes from the carpet construction itself:

- **Level loop** — uniform loop height. Clean, durable, the institutional default. Best wear recovery, lowest cost. Corridors, classrooms, general office.
- **Multi-level loop** — loops of two or more heights create sculpted texture and subtle patterning. Slightly softer appearance, still high-durability. Typical in corporate and education.
- **Patterned loop** — designed multi-level loop with a printed or woven graphic. Hospitality, branded corporate, wayfinding.
- **Cut pile (plush, velour, Saxony)** — loops cut at the top. Softer, more luxurious, shows footprints and vacuum tracks more readily. Executive, boardroom, hospitality.
- **Frieze** — tightly twisted cut pile; fibres curl, creating a pebbled texture that hides traffic lanes. Corporate, education.
- **Cut-and-loop (tip-sheared)** — combination of cut and loop pile in one construction, creating sculpted patterns. Hospitality, design-forward corporate.
- **Woven (Axminster, Wilton)** — fully woven goods (not tufted). Detailed multi-colour patterning, premium wear, premium cost. Ballrooms, theatres, heritage government.

## Best used for
- Government office floors (federal, provincial, municipal) — Class A and B
- University lecture halls, library reading rooms, faculty offices
- Hotel ballrooms, conference centres, convention space, pre-function corridors
- Theatres, auditoriums, places of worship, performance venues
- Long institutional corridors where seam control and continuous sightlines matter
- Executive suites, boardrooms, law-firm and financial-services offices
- Hospitality guestroom corridors (with entrance walk-off tied in)

**Ottawa context:** federal tenant-improvement work (PSPC / GC Workplace specs) still leans heavily on broadloom for ministerial offices, meeting rooms, and lecture-style spaces. Carleton and uOttawa continue to spec broadloom in reading rooms and lecture halls. The NAC, Shaw Centre, and major downtown hotels are core ballroom / pre-function broadloom clients.

## Best NOT used for
- Wet zones, kitchens, washrooms, janitor closets — sheet vinyl, welded sheet, or ceramic
- Healthcare clinical / procedural areas — sheet vinyl or rubber, for infection control and seamless cleanability
- Slab-on-grade with elevated moisture — test per ASTM F2170 first; if out of spec, remediate or choose a different material. A failed moisture call on broadloom is the single most expensive callback in the trade.
- Entry vestibules without a walk-off system — grit will chew out the face fibre within a season
- Open lab and shop floors — broadloom cannot take point loads, solvents, or wheeled carts

**GSA / US federal procurement** requires NSF/ANSI 140 Gold minimum on broadloom and tile. Canadian federal procurement (PSPC) frequently references the same standard and CRI Green Label Plus. Any commercial broadloom spec in 2026 should carry, at minimum, CRI Green Label Plus; competitive spec adds NSF 140 and a C2C material health certificate.

## Substrate prep requirements
- **Moisture testing** — mandatory on every concrete slab before glue-down per CRI 104:
  - **ASTM F1869 (calcium chloride / MVER)** — industry reference limit ≤ 3 lb / 1,000 ft² / 24 hr, unless the carpet or adhesive manufacturer publishes a higher tolerance (many moisture-tolerant adhesives are rated to 5–8 lb)
  - **ASTM F2170 (in-situ RH probe)** — industry reference limit ≤ 75% RH, unless manufacturer publishes higher (moisture-tolerant systems to 90% RH). F2170 is the preferred method on 2026 spec work because it measures internal slab equilibrium, not just surface emission; F1869 results cannot be mathematically converted to F2170
  - **pH testing** — most manufacturers require slab pH between 7 and 10 (some cap at 9). High-alkali slabs will saponify adhesive
  - Always defer to the **specific** manufacturer's published limits on the adhesive and the carpet — warranty voids cite these numbers exactly
- **Flatness** — ≤ 3/16" in 10 ft for commercial broadloom per CRI 104; tighter for woven goods
- **Patching** — cementitious patch compound (not gypsum) over cracks, control joints (honour live joints with a cover system), divots, and high spots
- **Cleanliness** — vacuum and damp-wipe; no curing compounds, sealers, or residual adhesives unless documented compatible
- **Adhesive compatibility check** — confirm the adhesive manufacturer's slab prep, moisture limits, and trowel notch against the carpet manufacturer's install guide. Mismatches here are warranty-voiding.

## IAQ / sustainability certifications to look for
On any commercial spec in 2026, the baseline is:
- **CRI Green Label Plus** on the carpet *and* the adhesive — carpet-specific low-emission standard
- **NSF/ANSI 140 Gold or Platinum** — full lifecycle sustainability rating (US GSA requires Gold minimum; Canadian federal spec frequently mirrors)
- **GREENGUARD Gold** — low chemical emissions, widely held across Shaw Contract, Mohawk Group, Tarkett, Mannington
- **Cradle to Cradle Certified (Bronze / Silver / Gold)** — material health and circularity; Shaw Contract EcoWorx and several Mohawk and Mannington lines carry this
- **FloorScore** — primarily a hard-surface certification, but some cushion-back broadlooms and adhesives carry it
- **Declare labels / HPDs (Health Product Declarations) / EPDs (Environmental Product Declarations)** — required for LEED v4.1, WELL, and increasingly for Canadian federal tenant-improvement work

For a LEED v4.1 / WELL v2 project: expect to submit CRI Green Label Plus certificates, NSF 140 certificate, HPD, EPD, and the adhesive's emission certification as part of the closeout submittal package.

## Maintenance
Reference: **CRI 204 — Commercial Carpet Maintenance and Cleaning Standard**. The IICRC and CRI jointly recognize five cleaning methods; hot-water extraction is the only one classified as true deep cleaning (reaches the full pile depth). The other four are interim / appearance methods.

**Daily**
- Dry vacuum with a CRI Seal of Approval vacuum (beater-bar on cut pile, suction-only on loop to avoid fuzzing)
- Immediate spot treatment of spills — blot, never rub; use pH-neutral spotter approved by the mill
- Walk-off mats at every entry (10–15 ft of matting captures ~80% of tracked-in grit)

**Interim (weekly to monthly in high-traffic zones)**
- **Encapsulation** — crystallizing polymer is brushed in with a low-moisture cylindrical machine, dries in 30–60 minutes, vacuumed the next day; minimal disruption, ideal for corridors and open office between deep cleans
- **Bonnet cleaning** — rotary machine with an absorbent pad and light cleaning solution; cleans the top third of the pile only, fast-dry; best for quick refreshes and light-soil cut-pile commercial
- **Dry-compound** — absorbent powder brushed in and vacuumed out; used where downtime must be zero

**Restorative (every 6–18 months depending on traffic)**
- **Hot-water extraction (HWE / "steam cleaning")** — the only true deep-cleaning method; pre-spray, agitate, inject hot rinse, vacuum-extract. Removes ~97% of particulate and biological soil. Drying time 4–8 hours with proper air movers. Required at least annually in heavy-traffic commercial per most mill warranties.

**Repair**
- Patch damaged areas with retained attic stock from the original install (always order 5–10% overage and label rolls)
- Re-secure lifted seams with seam iron and fresh seam sealer
- Stretch-in: re-stretch with power stretcher when perimeter waves appear (rare in glue-down)

**Typical commercial maintenance cycle:**
| Traffic level | Daily vacuum | Interim clean | Restorative HWE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light (executive, boardroom) | 3x / week | 6 months | 18–24 months |
| Moderate (general office) | Daily | 3 months | 12 months |
| Heavy (corridors, education, retail) | Daily, 2x peak | 4–6 weeks | 6–9 months |
| Very heavy (hospitality pre-function, convention) | 2x daily | 2–4 weeks | 3–6 months |

## Pricing ranges (CAD installed, 2026 — internal reference only, never quote)
Broadloom pricing is volatile in 2026 owing to ongoing nylon feedstock and transportation volatility. The ranges below are directional only; refresh every project against live mill quotes.

| Grade | Typical fibre / construction | Installed range (CAD/sq ft, material + labour + adhesive) |
|---|---|---|
| Budget / interim | Nylon 6 level loop, basic backing, running-line colourways | low end of the range |
| Standard commercial | Solution-dyed nylon 6, multi-level loop or patterned loop, running line | mid range |
| Premium commercial | Solution-dyed Antron-class nylon 6,6, designer running lines, NSF 140 Gold / C2C | upper-mid range |
| Custom / woven / designer | Custom-coloured tufted, Axminster or Wilton woven, hospitality and ballroom | high end, long lead |

**Labour and adhesive typically add meaningful cost beyond material** — moisture mitigation primer, patching, furniture lift / protection, after-hours work, and demo of existing flooring are all common extras on institutional projects. Assume 15–25% waste allowance on broadloom (pattern match, drop direction, cut loss) vs. 8–12% on tile.

## Lifecycle expectations
Realistic wear life in a well-maintained commercial setting:

| Grade | Class A office | Corridor / education | Hospitality pre-function |
|---|---|---|---|
| Budget interim | 5–7 years | 3–5 years | not recommended |
| Standard commercial | 10–12 years | 7–10 years | 5–7 years |
| Premium commercial (Antron-class, NSF 140 Gold) | 15–20 years | 10–15 years | 7–10 years |
| Custom / woven | 20+ years (heritage spaces) | 15+ years | 10–15 years |

Warranties from the major mills typically run 10–15 years on wear, 10 years on static, lifetime on delamination — but only if substrate prep, moisture testing, adhesive compatibility, and maintenance logs are all documented. Warranty claims without a moisture test report on file are routinely denied.
