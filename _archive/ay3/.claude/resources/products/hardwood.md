# Hardwood

## What it is

Solid and engineered wood flooring for showrooms, executive suites, boardrooms, faculty clubs, heritage restorations, and design-led commercial interiors. Specified site-finished for colour-match restorations or pre-finished with factory UV-cured aluminum-oxide / nanotech topcoats for faster occupancy turnover. Young Carpets Inc. installs new hardwood and maintains existing hardwood under scheduled service contracts across the National Capital Region — including after-hours and weekend screen-and-recoat work to keep occupied commercial tenants operational.

YCI's hardwood practice skews toward **Quebec-milled pre-finished engineered** product for new commercial installs (dimensional stability across the Ottawa seasonal humidity swing, and factory finishes warranted for commercial wear) and **site-finished solid white oak or red oak** for heritage and institutional match-backs.

---

## Types / sub-products

### 1. SOLID hardwood
- 3/4" (19 mm) nominal thickness, single species top to bottom
- Refinishable 3-5 times over its service life (full sand to bare wood)
- Nail-down only — requires a plywood subfloor
- Expands/contracts with ambient relative humidity; NOT suited to slab-on-grade or high-swing environments
- Best for: heritage restoration, wood-for-wood match-backs, university common rooms on plywood subfloors, executive suites in above-grade wood-framed construction
- Widths: 2-1/4", 3-1/4", 4" standard; 5-7" wide-plank available but risk of cupping rises with width

### 2. ENGINEERED hardwood
- Multi-ply cross-laminated construction (5-ply, 7-ply, or 11-ply common) with a real hardwood wear layer
- Wear layer thickness is the critical spec:
  - **2 mm** — residential / light commercial only, one light screen-and-recoat at most
  - **3-4 mm** — standard commercial spec, multiple screen-and-recoats, one light sand
  - **6 mm** — premium commercial, refinishable 2-3× like a thin solid
- Dimensionally stable — tolerates Ottawa's 20%-60% RH seasonal swing far better than solid
- Installs nail-down, full-spread glue-down, or click-lock floating
- Can go over concrete slab (with vapour control) — the main reason engineered dominates commercial spec
- Best for: above or below-grade commercial, tenant fit-outs, anything on slab

### 3. PARQUET / pattern
- Herringbone, chevron, basketweave, Versailles panels
- Available as solid blocks (traditional) or engineered (modern commercial spec)
- Pre-assembled Versailles and basketweave panels reduce install labour vs field-assembled herringbone
- Best for: hotel lobbies, boutique retail, design-forward corporate reception, heritage restoration
- Premium labour — plan 1.5-2× install hours vs straight-lay

## Install techniques

| Technique | Substrate | Sub-product | Adhesive / fastener | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Nail-down (cleat or staple) | Plywood subfloor ≥ 5/8" | Solid 3/4", some engineered | 1-1/2" to 2" cleats, 8-10" OC | Traditional; requires wood subfloor |
| Glue-down (full-spread) | Concrete or plywood | Engineered, parquet | Moisture-cure urethane only (Bostik, Mapei, Sika) | Never water-based adhesive for commercial |
| Click-lock floating | Any flat substrate | Engineered | No adhesive; foam/cork underlayment | Allows substrate movement; requires T-mouldings at doorways |
| Site-finished | Bare wood after install | Solid or engineered raw | Stain + 3 coats sealer/finish | Custom colour match; dust containment required |
| Pre-finished | n/a | Solid or engineered | Factory UV-cured aluminum-oxide / nanotech | Zero on-site VOC or dust; faster occupancy |

### NWFA substrate flatness tolerances
- **Glue-down / floating:** flat to 3/16" in 10' (or 1/8" in 6')
- **Nail-down:** flat to 1/4" in 10'

### Moisture testing (NWFA + ASTM)
- **ASTM F2170** — Relative humidity in-situ probe (the commercial standard; most accurate method for concrete slabs)
  - 3 test locations for the first 1,000 sq ft; 1 additional per 1,000 sq ft thereafter
  - Typical max RH for engineered glue-down: 75-85% (check adhesive datasheet)
- **ASTM F1869** — Calcium chloride moisture vapour emission rate (older; still referenced)
- **Moisture content differential:** NWFA requires ≤ 4% MC difference between subfloor and wood flooring before install
- **Site conditions at install:** 60-80°F, 30-50% RH — same as service-life RH
- **Acclimatization:** minimum 14 days on-site in controlled environment; confirmed with a pin moisture meter, not by calendar

## Species (common commercial spec in Ontario)

### Janka hardness reference (lbf, side-hardness)

| Species | Janka | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Hickory | 1,820 | Hardest common domestic; dramatic grain variation |
| Hard / Sugar Maple | 1,450 | Tight grain, pale colour, stains unevenly; gymnasium standard |
| White Oak | 1,360 | Closed grain, ring-porous, takes stain evenly; commercial favourite |
| White Ash | 1,320 | Similar grain to oak, slightly lighter tone |
| Red Oak | 1,290 | Open grain, pinker tone; heritage-match standard in Ottawa institutional buildings |
| Yellow Birch | 1,260 | Pale, uniform; Quebec-grown, common in Canadian engineered product |
| Black Walnut | 1,010 | Softer; specified for executive design appeal, not wear |
| Eastern White Pine | 380 | Heritage only — not a commercial wear spec |

(Janka values cross-referenced against NWFA-aligned tables, Hoadley *Understanding Wood*, and The Wood Database.)

### Which species for which environment
- **Heavy commercial wear (lobbies, corridors, retail):** hard maple, hickory, or white oak with a commercial-rated finish
- **Executive / boardroom:** white oak (rift & quartered for dimensional stability), walnut for design
- **Heritage match-back (Ottawa pre-1970 buildings):** red oak — the dominant species in Ontario/Quebec institutional construction
- **Gymnasiums and active sport:** hard maple (MFMA grade — separate spec, not covered here)
- **Design-forward / hospitality:** European white oak, wire-brushed, reactive-stained

### Sustainability / certification
- **FSC** (Forest Stewardship Council) — chain-of-custody certified product available from all four major Quebec mills below
- **NWFA Responsible Procurement Program (RPP)** — NWFA's own sourcing verification
- **CARB Phase 2** — formaldehyde emission limit for engineered plywood cores (0.05 ppm); all reputable North American engineered product complies
- **FloorScore** — IAQ certification for finished flooring products
- **EPD** (Environmental Product Declarations) — Preverco and Mirage both publish EPDs for LEED v4 MR credits

## Substrate prep

### Over plywood subfloor
- Subfloor ≥ 5/8" CDX or T&G OSB (APA rated)
- Flat to 1/4" in 10' for nail-down; 3/16" in 10' for glue-down or floating
- Moisture content of plywood within 4% of flooring MC at install
- Acclimatize flooring in the space for 14+ days with HVAC running at service-life conditions

### Over concrete slab
- Full moisture test per ASTM F2170 before committing to engineered glue-down
- Slab flatness 3/16" in 10'; grind high spots, patch low spots with Portland-based self-leveller
- Vapour retarder required — either a vapour-retarding urethane adhesive (Bostik Greenforce, Mapei Ultrabond ECO 995, Sika SikaBond) OR a separate 6-mil poly under a floating install
- Never install solid 3/4" directly to concrete (no method accepted) — engineered only below-grade and on-grade

### Humidity control
- Continuous HVAC operation for at least 5 days before install, during install, and permanently after occupancy
- Service-life RH 30-50% — the Ottawa winter dry-down is the dominant failure mode for under-acclimated wood

## Best used for

- Commercial showrooms, executive suites, boardrooms, C-suite reception
- University faculty clubs, alumni halls, donor lounges, heritage libraries
- Heritage building restoration (Parliamentary Precinct, ByWard Market, Sandy Hill, the Glebe)
- Hotel and boutique hospitality lobbies
- Retail flagships with a design budget
- Above-grade or on-grade (engineered) tenant fit-outs with controlled HVAC

## Best NOT used for

- Healthcare clinical zones — use sheet vinyl or rubber (infection control and cleanability)
- Kitchens, washrooms, locker rooms, wet mechanical areas
- Slab-on-grade without vapour control or moisture testing
- Spaces without continuous HVAC (seasonal cottages, unheated storage, warehouse)
- High-humidity swing environments — never solid; engineered only with documented RH control
- Rolling-load floors (library stacks on casters, hospital bed rooms) — use LVT or sheet vinyl
- Direct sunlight exposure without UV window film (all hardwood ambers/fades — oak to warm tones, maple to yellow)

## MSDS / SDS resources

- **Site-finished projects:** stain + sealer + topcoat SDS from manufacturer (Bona, Loba, Pallmann, Rubio Monocoat). YCI installer kit should carry current SDS for every product on the truck for that day's scope
- **Urethane adhesives:** Bostik, Mapei, Sika — SDS from each manufacturer's technical portal
- **Pre-finished:** factory UV-cured finish is fully cured and inert at install; no installer-use SDS required
- **Sanding dust:** site-finish and refinish work generates combustible wood dust — NFPA 664 / CCOHS dust-control procedures apply; HEPA vacuum containment is the commercial standard of care

## Maintenance

### Daily / routine (facility staff)
- Dust mop or vacuum (hard-floor head, no beater bar)
- Walk-off matting at all entries (minimum 12' of matting inside and outside combined)
- Spot-clean spills immediately — wood is not water-tolerant

### Periodic (facility staff or YCI contract)
- Damp mop with NWFA-approved hardwood cleaner (Bona Pro, Pallmann Clean)
- Never use vinegar, ammonia, steam, or general-purpose floor soap — all will haze the finish and void manufacturer warranties
- Furniture glides on all rolling and static furniture; replace annually

### Restorative — screen-and-recoat (YCI service)
- Frequency: every 3-7 years depending on traffic
- Process: abrade the existing finish with a 120-grit screen on a buffer, vacuum, apply 1-2 new topcoats
- **Does not remove stain or repair deep scratches** — it refreshes the finish film only
- Commercial engineered product with a factory UV finish requires a manufacturer-approved bonding primer before site-recoat (Bona, Loba, Pallmann all make these)
- After-hours and weekend scheduling is YCI's standard offer for occupied commercial tenants — National Capital Region coverage

### Full refinish (YCI service)
- Frequency: every 10-20 years for solid and 6mm-wear-layer engineered; never for thin-wear-layer engineered
- Process: sand to bare wood (3-grit sequence typ. 36 / 60 / 100), optional stain, 3 coats finish
- Requires floor evacuation and dust containment; 3-5 days minimum turnaround
- Cannot refinish engineered product with < 3 mm wear layer remaining

### YCI maintenance contracts
- Scheduled screen-and-recoat on 3, 5, or 7-year cycles
- After-hours / weekend execution for occupied commercial tenants
- Service area: Ottawa, Gatineau, National Capital Region, Eastern Ontario
